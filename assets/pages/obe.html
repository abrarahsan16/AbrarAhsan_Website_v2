OBE Stars

Artistic Work for 2018 ESCH Awards Ceremony

OBE stars is a collection of artistic work that complements and celebrates student work at the 2018 ESCH Awards Ceremony.

Inspired by Japanese origami, the triangulated components act as a base form for a series of custom lighting products.

The project was designed and implemented by myself, Vishal Mehra, Ernest Wong and Agnes Yuen.

My contribution to the project is on the software and electronics side, along with Vishal Mehra, while Agnes and Ernest worked on the physical design and implementation.

The design proposal is found in the document here. Personal contribution is listed below.

Objective

Using a microphone and Arduino microcontroller, a responsive system is created that visualizes the frequency spectrum of the venue and displays it via LED lights within the sphere. The light diffuses through the cut patterns on the surface of the sphere. The greater the sound is detected through the microphone, the light and pattern is intensified.

Multiple pieces were made, some for the stages, others for the wall and tables. The overall objective was to beautify the event with artistic work. Both wall and table pieces also had a built-in Arduino to alternate colours on a fixed schedule, unlike the stage pieces which were responsive to audio.

Stage Pieces

The internals of the stage piece is a cube with LED stripes spread across evenly. Each strip is soldered to a proto-board to allow quick replacement if parts appear faulty.

An Arduino Mega was used, with the microphone placed near the top through a gap to ensure it gets the maximum audio reception during the ceremony.

Whenever the speakers would talk on stage, the input from the microphone will trigger the Arduino to alternate the colours on the LED to give it a "reactive"-ness.
